Venice, Italy

Current and past impact of climate change on Venice

Venice is a city in Italy, famous for its nickname "The City of Canals" and its vast network of bridges and canals. Rising sea levels have caused Venice to experience frequent flooding, which is often intense and disrupts daily life. In addition to the rising sea levels, Venice is also sinking. Venice is sinking due to both the compression of the land it was built on--soft soil--, and the fact that the buildings are very heavy.

Future impact of climate change on Venice

Melting glaciers will continue to cause sea levels to rise. Rising sea levels and strong storms will continue to cause heavy floods. These floods leave behind salt, which damages things like art and buildings in the city. The city is likely to be submerged sometime before 2150.

Ways to minimize the impact of climate change

  • Be mindful of water use
    • Don't take long showers
    • Turn off the water while brushing teeth
    • Only run dishwashers/washing machines with full loads
  • Try to eat less red meat
  • Reuse things when possible
  • Bring your own bags to the store to avoid using plastic ones

What are the people in Venice trying to do?

  • They are encouraging sustainable tourism by promoting things like environmentally friendly transport.
  • They are trying to restore things in nature that can act as natural buffers or safety nets for the city, like marshes.
  • They built the MOSE flood barriers, which are designed to protect against high tides.
